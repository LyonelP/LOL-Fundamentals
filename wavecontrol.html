Wave Control Overview

Bronze

Usually hits the wave randomly and doesn’t think about where the wave will end up. Often pushes accidentally or misses opportunities to crash the wave.

  • Rarely thinks about wave states.
  • Focuses mostly on last hitting.
  • May stay in lane too long with bad recalls.

Platinum

Understands basic slow pushing and crashing. Starts recognizing when to push for recalls or roams.

  • Can build larger waves.
  • Uses cannon waves for recalls.
  • Starts thinking about jungle pressure.

Challenger

The goal of wave control is usually to crash the wave with your minions as healthy as possible. Most high elo wave control revolves around pushing efficiently while staying safe.

  • Understands push timings and crash windows.
  • Uses cannon waves to create tempo advantages.
  • Controls recalls, roams, and vision through wave states.

Early Game

Bronze

  • Usually doesn’t intentionally pull waves.
  • Gets ganked often while pushing randomly.
  • May trade into large enemy waves.

Platinum

  • Starts slow pushing to trade safely.
  • Understands that bigger enemy waves pull toward you.
  • Uses wave position to avoid jungle pressure.

Challenger

Early game wave control is usually based around pulling and slow pushing. This allows safer trading, zoning, and protection from ganks.

  • Mid lane usually only needs +1 enemy minion to pull in center lane.
  • Closer to tower requires more enemy minions.
  • Understands exact pull thresholds by lane length and position.

Maintaining Wave Position

Bronze

  • Lets waves crash too often.
  • Usually resets the wave accidentally.

Platinum

  • Can hold waves near tower temporarily.
  • Starts tanking waves outside tower range.

Challenger

High elo players maintain wave position by leaving one enemy minion alive and tanking it outside tower range.

  • Prevents unwanted crashes.
  • Creates safe trading positions.
  • Controls lane spacing and recall timings.

Mid Game

Bronze

  • Leaves waves in bad positions before roaming.
  • Recalls without crashing waves.

Platinum

  • Pushes before roaming more consistently.
  • Starts using fast pushes to deny CS.

Challenger

Mid game wave control becomes heavily push-focused because most champions clear waves faster.

  • Always crashes before roaming or recalling.
  • Uses fast pushing to maximize CS denial.
  • Controls tempo through wave speed.

Freezing

Bronze

  • Rarely freezes intentionally.
  • Usually breaks freezes by overpushing.

Platinum

  • Can freeze in certain matchups.
  • Uses freezes more against melee champions.

Challenger

Freezing is less common mid due to faster game pace and larger XP range, but still extremely strong in some matchups.

  • Strong against melee champions.
  • Useful against champions with weak roaming or jungle clear.
  • Understands exact freeze thresholds.

Late Game

Bronze

  • Often ignores side waves completely.
  • Groups too early without pushing.

Platinum

  • Starts pushing side lanes before objectives.
  • Recognizes mid priority importance.

Challenger

Late game wave control is usually simple: push mid as fast as possible to gain deep vision and objective control.

  • Uses wave priority to set up picks.
  • Creates vision pressure before objectives.
  • Controls map tempo through mid push.
