Wave Control

The goal of wave control is to crash your wave into the enemy tower with your minions as healthy as possible. It's actually almost always best to push, if you can do so safely, quickly, and without spending too many resources.

Cannon waves take much longer to clear and are harder to fast push. Pulling the wave before a cannon wave arrives is often the best strategy.

Early Game Wave Control

In the early game, pulling the wave and slow pushing is the most common because it allows you to trade, zone your opponent, and play with the safety of your tower. It's also the time when you’re most likely to be ganked since you have to stay in lane longer to last hit.

To pull the wave, the enemy wave must be larger. The number of extra enemy minions needed depends on where the wave currently is. The closer the wave is to your tower, the more enemy minions are needed because your minions arrive sooner.

  • Mid lane needs the least minions because it’s the shortest lane.
  • In the center of lane, around +1 enemy minion is enough to pull.
  • Near your tower, you need about +3 enemy minions.

Maintaining Wave Position

You can keep the wave close to your tower without needing a large enemy wave by killing all enemy minions except one and tanking that last minion just outside tower range. This prevents the wave from crashing and creates a controlled position to trade safely.

Mid Game Wave Control

In the mid game, pushing becomes the default pattern because it becomes easier for most champions to clear waves quickly while still trading.

When you push the enemy out of lane and want to recall or roam, always push the wave fully into the tower. A large wave can finish the last few minions on its own if needed.

The faster you push, the more CS the enemy loses to tower because their minions reach the wave sooner when they return.

Freezing

Freezing isn’t very common in mid lane anymore due to faster game pace, large XP range, and difficulty tanking waves. Still, it's strong in some matchups:

  • Against melee champions who can’t walk up safely.
  • Against champions who struggle to roam or clear jungle camps.

Tips

  • You can freeze behind the side bush wall to make last hitting easier.
  • In top and bot lane you can step into the side bushes to drop minion aggro, just don't let the minions move too far up.

Late Game Strategy

Late game wave control is simple: push mid as fast as possible. Fast pushing gives your team deep vision and lets you set up picks, objectives, and map control.
