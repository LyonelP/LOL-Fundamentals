Wave Control Overview

The goal of wave control is to crash your wave into the enemy tower with your minions as healthy as possible. Pushing safely and efficiently is almost always the best choice.

Cannon waves take longer to clear, so pulling the wave before they arrive is often optimal.

Early Game Wave Control

Pulling and slow pushing allows trades, zoning, and safer positioning.

  • Pulling the wave requires more enemy minions the closer you are to your tower.
  • Mid lane: least minions needed.
  • Center lane: +1 enemy minion sufficient.
  • Near tower: +3 enemy minions needed.

Maintaining Wave Position

Keep the wave near your tower by leaving one enemy minion alive and tanking it just outside tower range. This prevents the wave from crashing and allows safe trades.

Mid Game Wave Control

Pushing becomes standard. Large waves crash towers faster and allow you to recall or roam without losing resources.

The faster you push, the more CS the enemy loses when returning to lane.

Freezing

Freezing is situational and effective against melee champions or champions who struggle to roam. Tips:

  • Freeze behind side bushes to make last hitting easier.
  • Step into bushes to drop minion aggro, but don’t let the wave push too far.

Late Game Strategy

Push mid as fast as possible to provide deep vision and map control. Fast waves allow better setup for picks, objectives, and team pressure.
