Understanding the Early Game

The early game is arguably the most crucial phase of League of Legends, as it sets the foundation for how the rest of the match will unfold. Level 1 is particularly important because the game can begin to snowball immediately from this point. This is why I always make sure to use my ward at level 1 or level 2 to prevent early casualties and maintain lane control.

This phase generally lasts until around the 14-minute mark. During this period, every trade, jungle path, and objective attempt can have significant consequences on the mid and late game. Success here often determines whether your team gains an early advantage or falls behind.

Key focuses during the early game include:

  • Vision Control: Place wards strategically to avoid early ganks and secure objectives like Scuttle Crab and Rift Herald.
  • Safe Trading: Take calculated trades to maintain health advantage without overextending.
  • Jungle Awareness: Track the enemy jungler’s likely path to avoid ambushes and set up counter plays.
  • Objective Priority: Early dragons, Rift Herald, and turret plates can define momentum for your team.

Mastering these early game fundamentals ensures that you enter the mid game with a strong position and the tools necessary to carry the rest of the match.
