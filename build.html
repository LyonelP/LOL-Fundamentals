Build

Figure out your core build before you queue up. Follow champion guides from high elo players who specialize in your champion. Having your item build pre-planned increases your buy speed and reduces unnecessary time spent in the fountain. It’s also important to know which component items are optimal at different gold thresholds in each situation.

I also recommend Lolalytics for builds.

Adjusting Your Build in Champion Select

Once in champion select, you may need to tweak your core build depending on the enemy team composition. Consider the amount of magic damage, physical damage, healing, assassins, and tanks.

  • If your lane opponent deals mostly magic damage and the enemy jungler does as well, an early Null-Mantle is almost always a good choice for top, mid, or jungle. This can later be built into Mercury's Treads for additional utility.
  • AP champions often have more crowd control than AD champions, so adjust your build for tenacity or sustain as needed.

Build Philosophy

Most champions have set core items. The only parts of the build that often change are boots. Mercury's Treads is extremely strong since MR is roughly 3 times more efficient than armor early. For AP champions, building penetration boots is almost always better than Plated Steelcaps—even against a full AD team—because armor mainly lets AD champions play aggressively without losing much damage. Meanwhile, AP champions benefit more from penetration to maintain high damage output.

When to Build Penetration

Consider the scaling of MR and armor per level:

  • MR scaling reduces the effectiveness of magic damage; pen items or boots are more efficient against squishy or mid-level MR targets.
  • Armor scaling similarly reduces physical damage efficiency, but is often easier to outscale with armor penetration or lethality items.
  • Building penetration is generally better than building resistances early unless the enemy has extremely high damage and your team lacks peel.
