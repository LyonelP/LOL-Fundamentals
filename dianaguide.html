Build

Starting Items

  • Doran’s Ring: The most efficient item in the game and it gives the perfect stats for laning.
  • Dark Seal: I always buy this unless I can build a full Stormsurge on my first buy. The health, AP, and snowball potential is exactly what Diana needs. It’s worth it even though I rarely upgrade it to Mejai’s Soulstealer.
  • Cloth Armor: I’ll purchase this after Dark Seal or if I only have 300 gold on the first back and I’m against an AD mid/jg combo like Qiyana/Graves.
  • Null Magic Mantle: This is one of the most efficient items in the game outside of the Doran’s items. You should always look to pick it up against AP mid/jungle duos or into poke champions like Hwei. Magic resistance is especially strong versus poke because poke champions have longer cooldowns, and rely on sustained chip damage to control the lane.
  • Mercury Treads: If an early null magic mantle was purchased, it’s often upgraded into merc treads unless it’s a low CC AP Mid JG combo like Azir and Nidalee. Tenacity is an important stat for Diana but I’ll mostly wait for Elixir of Iron to gain tenacity since magic pen boots are even more important at the moment and MR can be gained through Banshee's Veil.
  • Sorcerer's Shoes: This is Diana’s go to boot now. Diana’s ratios have been buffed but she still heavily relies on her base damages.
  • Ninja Tabi: Always build sorcerer shoes instead of tanks even against full ad team compositions. Ad champions inherently have high dps making defensive options against them worse. The combination of pen boots plus early Hourglass is significantly better.

Core Items

  • Stormsurge: Diana’s best early damage spike, with AP, magic penetration, and movement speed.
  • Hourglass: This is Diana’s most core item after her rework. It’s almost always a second purchase, and can even be rushed against specific AD mid/jungle combinations like Zed/Rek’Sai. Around this timing, Diana naturally out-DPSes most AP champions, and Hourglass lets her negate their key abilities, putting her in a winning position. It’s also excellent for dropping tower aggro during dives.
  • Banshee’s Veil: This is a core part of Diana’s build now since she almost always opts for magic penetration boots. It’s the best way for Diana to gain MR, helps guarantee a combo/engage and makes her harder to pick off.
  • Death Cap: Diana’s ratios have been heavily nerfed but Deathcap is still a good late game option after Hourglass and Banshees. If I’m sitting on 2300 gold with a large rod already purchased, I might grab it before Banshee’s since the next component can easily be a Negatron Cloak. When deciding between Death Cap and Cryptbloom or Void staff, I’ll try to buy Deathcap, if I have enough gold for the large rod buys, and there are still carries on the enemy team without MR. As soon as enemies buy MR, they pretty much go off Diana’s radar.
  • Cryptbloom: I think Cryptbloom is slightly superior to void staff unless everyone on the enemy team has purchased one or more magic resistance items. Diana almost never wants to target enemies with MR and having void staff or cryptbloom doesn’t change that. Having additional haste and a heal that can swing a team fight makes a much larger difference

Elixirs

  • Elixir of Iron: I’ll almost always opt for this elixir instead of sorcery for the tenacity and health
  • Elixir of Sorcery: Helps Diana push towers even faster. I won’t start buying Elixirs on Diana until very late though because she has so many core items to complete.

Standard Full Build

Example Gold Buys

  • 500 Gold: Dark Seal + Refillable Potion
  • 1000 Gold: Dark Seal + Refillable Potion + Amplifying Tome + Control Ward (Pink Ward)
  • 1100 Gold: Alternator vs Pen Boots: If I’m sitting on tier-one boots with 800 gold and need to choose between Hextech Alternator or upgrading to magic penetration boots, the decision usually depends on whether I need to get back to lane quickly.

`, "Abilities": `

Abilities

Passive — Moonsilver Blade

Diana’s passive has changed from a burst tool to an attack speed dps tool. The Ratio is only .5 and Diana’s kit doesn’t enable her to get too many auto attacks off so it's mostly to boost early game trades, and take objectives. Diana’s main source of damage. The passive has high base damage and a great AP ratio of .8.

Q — Crescent Strike

Great for pushing from range. Diana q reveals targets and can be used to check an area and enable r dashes.
Tip: Diana can q flash. This combo is good when you need two r dashes to kill the opponent or when going into a fight against multiple enemies.

W — Pale Cascade

W gives an instant shield and three orbs that can be proced for damage. Pricing all 3 resets the shield.
Tip: Avoid using w when you’re unable to proc all orbs.
Tip: 1 orb can be used to safely last hit and the remaining two can be used to thin out the wave or to trade with the enemy.
Tip: If the enemy can block one of w orbs from the first use than w won’t go on cooldown for around 5 seconds and Diana won’t receive the second shield. This is common against Yasuo’s windwall.

E — Lunar Rush

Quick targeted engage. E will reset if the target has moonlight from q on them. E also resets now if a minion dies from q if the e was cast immediately afterwards. Casting E places you behind your target. You can auto attack at the end of the animation as well.

R — Moonfall

AOE ability and knockup that is great for teamfights, and catching out opponents. The pull and slow also help Diana utilize her passive or guarantee a q. Can be casted during E, and be animation canceled with E. Quick targeted engage. Diana R can be used to dodge skillshots.
Tip: Diana can R flash. Diana R also interrupts channels and dashes although the window to cancel a dash is smaller now and needs to be casted at the start of their dash.

`, "Combos": `

Combos

Stack your passive before a combo. Diana is often strongest when there are more targets around to stack her passive on. This includes minion waves, jungle camps, and even enemy towers.

  • Q E W AA E: This is the most basic combo.

  • R W Q E AA E: This combo is best, when you’re enemy is in E range. R almost guarantees for the Q to land.

  • Q AA E AA: This combo is best for extended trades.

  • E W AA Q: Quick burst combo but gives the enemy a chance to retaliate, if the combo didn’t do enough damage.

  • Passive E W AA Q: Better variation than the one above that most champions wont be able to trade back against.

Tips:

  • Diana R can interrupt dashes. If you're not going to get an E reset this becomes even more important. If they have a dash available you normally want to E immediately after R since they are most likely to use their dash during that time. If they wait to dash you'll still have applied the knock up and the slow.

  • When chasing an enemy down a lane, check the minion waves in that lane on the minimap so you know when you can Q E to the minion wave to catch up.

  • Use Diana W when right next to your target to maximize her damage. Diana W can also be used during E so its normally best to can activate it right before you land.

  • The moonlight applied by Diana's Q lasts 3 seconds. Waiting to use your R until right before Moonlight fades will allow you to get more dashes off, as another Q will be available soon.

  • Diana can cast R right before she lands her E.

  • It's good to use R at the beginning of your burst combo to almost guarantee the Q.

Level 1:

First thing you want to do is check your opponents items and see if they didn’t buy a Doran's item. Not having a Doran's item makes your opponent extremely vulnerable to all ins so the main goal is to keep them in lane until level 3. I’ll trade slightly less until then to make sure I’m healthy enough to go for the all-in. If they bought a Doran's item, I’ll look for a Q as soon as I can. I’ll move up with the ranged creeps and try to get a read on their movement as they move up to the wave. Look to see if they're looking to trade with you immediately, if they’re looking to push, or if they’re waiting for you to throw your q. Throwing the first q starts your mana regeneration but q’s afterwards should only be thrown when they’re guaranteed to land which should be close to off cooldown. If the enemy is tankier, it's often best to wait until you can gain a Manaflow Band stack from landing a q. If the enemy is pushing the wave you want to sync your autos on the melee creeps with their auto attacks on the wave to safely thin out the wave.

Level 2:

Take W level 2 unless the enemy is below half health and even if you’re considering taking E wait to level it up until your Q is going to hit them. If it’s a standard lane, use W to thin out the wave in ranged matchups and to trade aggressively with grasp and autos in melee matchups. Continue to look for Q’s but save the cooldown for the level 3 power spike. If your q’s hit a lot of the minion wave it's often best to crash this second non canon wave. It’s better to avoid doing this though so you can take advantage of your level 3 power spike and buy timings. Ideally have your q’s hit as few minions as possible unless the enemy is pushing the wave with their abilities as well. This will lead the wave to being on your side of the lane. If the enemy isn’t a ranged ad champion then you can also try pulling the wave and using w to do so if necessary.

Level 3:

If you crashed the wave level 2, then you want to try to auto the first melee creep if they let you. This helps hit level 3 at the same time as your opponent if not earlier. The wave will push back your way and this is when the enemy jungler will be looking to gank you so it’s often best to play safe until the wave is back in your side of the lane. Once the wave is on your side you want to try to keep grasp up with autos in the wave right as it’s about to fall off and looks for trades with passive grasp and E W Auto Q or Q E W Auto Autothen wait for them to flash and your grasp to be back up for an E auto or a flash E auto. Level 6:

At level 6 you get your R look for all-ins post-6 with proper combo timing and jungle coordination.

Level 10:

If you have prio look to push the incoming minion wave as soon as possible. If the lane is more even and you have to be worried about poke or a gank then stay in fog of ward until the wave is about to settle to come out and q the wave.

Level 11:

Farming / Pushing

Diana is one of the best farming champs in the game. She clears both waves and jungle camps extremely quickly. After level 5 Diana can two Q the ranged creeps. This is Diana's main way of getting prio in lane because she can use all the minions to close onto the enemy. Most enemies also won't be able to match this push.

Farming Combos

Q the wave, E through a melee minion or the canon minion if there is one, using W to finish of the ranged creeps, and turning towards the melee creeps to finish them off with passive.

Farming Tip number 1: Farming under tower early game: Diana's passive will perfectly prep the ranged creeps hit by the AOE. Stack her passive from the melee creeps and wait for the first ranged creep to be hit by a tower shot, then use your passive on that creep and simply auto the remaining creeps after the tower shots. Each Diana passive stack and the passive last 3.5 seconds.

Farming Tip number 2: Diana’s W is great for assisting with last hits. Early game each sphere is about one auto and you can kite back between each sphere. The spheres last 5 seconds as well as the shield if proced.

Threats & Synergies

Threats

Any champion with high sustain damage and survivability counters Diana. Diana has to commit to the fight and if she can’t burst or out dps the enemy champion she’ll die.

Counters

Common counters include Yasuo, Irelia, and Sylas. Diana is the most vulnerable to champions with high sustain damage and champions that are hard to kill. Diana doesn't have the sustain damage anymore or the mobility to consistently outduel those champions once they reach key items. Most top lane champions counter Diana. However, it’s possible to beat them before they buy MR.

Synergies

Diana synergizes well with champions that can follow her engage or get her out of tough situations. She also does best with champions who aren’t vulnerable to engages and can stall for her to split push or to find a flank.

  • Thresh: Since he can pull Diana back out of the fight when necessary.

  • Yasuo: The ult combo is good but having Yasuo bot or top just for the combo isn’t necessarily worth it.

  • Camille: (works with coordinated engages).

Bad synergies

Having multiple splitpushers on a team is never ideal.

  • Fiora: Having multiple split pushers isn’t ideal and she can’t follow up your engage well or engage herself.

  • Tryndamere: Same as Fiora.

Pros

  • Burst: Diana has some of the fastest burst in the game, and it's very reliable.
