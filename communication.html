Communication

The best way to communicate with your team is through pings. I’ve found that pinging twice and checking to see if the communication was received before pinging again works best. Chat is best used for keeping your team morale high. Time spent typing is time not spent playing, so it’s best to minimize it.

Typing “gj” after someone makes a good play is great, and “ty” if someone hands over a buff to you. When you’re behind or the enemy makes a good play, typing “np we’re fine” or “we’ll win” works wonders. Using the thumbs-up emote instead of typing “gj” is also effective and faster.

Common Pings

Generic Ping: Used to ping everything in the tab window like summoner spells, ults, and upcoming objectives. Also great for marking enemy objectives.

Danger: Warns teammates of enemy roams, ganks, possible engages, flanks, TPs, and incoming abilities.

Assist Me: I need help securing an objective or winning/surviving a fight.

On My Way: Only ping when you’re close to joining the fight.

Enemy Missing (MIA): Alerts teammates to play safe because an enemy is no longer in vision. Ping once if the enemy is unlikely to commit to the roam and twice or more when it is likely.

Warded: Area needs vision for your team or the enemy team has warded that area.

Push: Push the lane to crash the wave. Mostly used after a gank and when low on resources.
