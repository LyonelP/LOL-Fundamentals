Laning Fundamentals

In LoL you always want to focus on what you can control. The three most important actions are your movement clicks on the map, your auto attacks and your abilities. These inputs make up the largest majority of your laning but also your general skill level. The difference between an iron player and a challenger player comes down to the timings and placement of these clicks.

Goal of Laning:
Farm every minion while denying your opponent as many as possible, all while maximizing damage to your opponent and their tower.

Mental Stack

  • Enemy Position Where are they positioned relative the lane, minions, and players, and are they on a forward or back step?
  • Wave State – How fast is the wave pushing or pulling? Has the wave settled yet and which minions are low
  • Enemy & Teammate Position – Jungler pathing, and roams from top and bot lane.
  • Cooldowns – What abilities are available? Which are down on for how long?
  • Resources – Health, mana, potions, and sustain.
  • Runes – Proc windows like Electrocute, Conqueror, Bone Plating.
  • Items – Item spikes and component advantages.
  • Gold – When do and your opponent want to recall and with how much gold?

If you are unsure what to do, start at the top of the stack and work down.

Levels 1–6

Level 1

Your initial movement is the most important as you’re limited to ~1 ability. It’s crucial you move with the wave and specifically the ranged creeps. Then this is when you’ll look to make your first action with an auto attack or ability. Abilities should always be prioritized and only be used on minions as a last resort. An exception is when you can use an ability to last hit like Annie Q and trade with Auto Attacks. Abilities can be used to bait out enemy spells as well if they expect you to Auto Attack to last hit. This will at least trade abilities evenly.
