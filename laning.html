Laning Fundamentals

Goals of Laning

The goal of League is to destroy the enemy Nexus and therefore the goal of laning is to destroy the tier 1 tower. This is achieved by forcing your opponent out of lane by a reset or a kill. This allows you and your minions to reach the enemy tower. The more gold you gain from minions and the more you deny from your opponent, the easier it is to reach and stay at the enemy tower.

General Rules of Laning

  • The stronger you are in lane, the less you want to roam. The enemy team needs to come to you to stop you from pressuring the tower and laner.
  • As a melee champion, wait for the wave to settle before stepping up.
  • Build resists based on both your lane opponent and the enemy jungler. A null mantle against an AP Mid/Jungle combo and a cloth armor against AD (less important against AD).
  • Tower plate or tempo? If you went for a tower plate, then you have to be able to stay for at least one additional wave since you’ll lose too much tempo otherwise.

Mental Stack

  • Enemy Position – Where are they positioned relative to the lane, minions, and players? Are they stepping forward or backward?
  • Wave State – How fast is the wave pushing or pulling? Has it settled, is it a canon wave, and when does the next wave arrive?
  • Minions – Which minions do you and your opponent need to last hit and how will you and them go about it.
  • Enemy & Teammate Position – Jungle pathing and potential roams from top and bot lane.
  • Cooldowns – What abilities are available? Which ones are down, and for how long?
  • Resources – Health, mana, potions, and sustain.
  • Vision – What vision do you and your enemy have around the lane.
  • Runes – Proc windows like Electrocute, Conqueror, and Bone Plating.
  • Items – Item spikes and component advantages.
  • Gold – When do you and your opponent want to recall, and with how much gold?

If you are unsure what to do, start at the top of the stack and work down.
